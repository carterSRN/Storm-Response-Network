Mission & Purpose

Information that helps people act.

Our mission is to strengthen community resilience through responsible weather reporting, clear safety communication, emergency preparedness, resilient communications, and organized support for communities affected by severe weather.

01

Observe

Encourage trained, location-specific observations of hail, wind, flooding, damage, and dangerous storm structure.

02

Communicate

Share clear information and direct the public toward official watches, warnings, and instructions.

03

Prepare

Promote emergency planning, weather-radio use, go-kits, first-aid readiness, and practical severe-weather safety.

04

Support

Connect members and donors with organized community assistance and reputable disaster-relief efforts.

Core Operations

Operating Standards

Safety. Accuracy. Integrity.

SRN members are expected to communicate responsibly, avoid exaggeration, respect incident command, and never represent themselves as government personnel or public-safety officers without authorization.

Safety First

No report, photo, video, or chase is worth placing the public or a member in danger.

Verify Before Posting

Separate confirmed observations from interpretation, estimates, and unverified reports.

Use Official Channels

Urgent life-safety information should be reported through appropriate official channels whenever possible.

Respect Authority

Follow road closures, law-enforcement direction, emergency-management instructions, and incident-command structure.

Protect Privacy

Avoid sharing sensitive personal information, victim imagery, or operational details that could cause harm.

No False Affiliation

Never imply SRN is NOAA, NWS, FEMA, law enforcement, fire, EMS, or another government organization.
